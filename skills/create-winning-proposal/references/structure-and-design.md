# Structure and design

Always use the buyer's mandatory outline first. Otherwise choose only the sections needed.

## Public/RFP proposal

1. Executive summary
2. Requirement response or compliance matrix
3. Understanding of the requirement
4. Proposed solution and architecture
5. Delivery method, schedule, and deliverables
6. Governance, staffing, and responsibilities
7. Operations, support, security, quality, and compliance
8. Risks, assumptions, dependencies, and exclusions
9. Outcomes, metrics, and acceptance
10. Commercial response and evidence appendices when requested

## Private sales proposal

1. Decision summary
2. Current situation and impact
3. Proposed outcome and scope
4. Approach and differentiators
5. Delivery plan and responsibilities
6. Value, measures, risks, and commercial terms
7. Next decision

## Technical proposal

1. Summary and goals
2. Requirements and non-goals
3. Current state and constraints
4. Proposed design
5. Security, operations, data, and migration
6. Alternatives and trade-offs
7. Delivery, testing, acceptance, and rollback
8. Risks and open decisions

## Page design

- Give each page or major section one dominant message.
- Use Word heading styles with a valid hierarchy; never simulate headings only with bold text.
- Apply brand fonts and colors only from supplied assets.
- Use tables for repeated mappings, not decorative layout.
- Label units, dates, scope, data source, and baseline on charts and tables.
- Provide captions and text alternatives for meaningful figures.
- Pair color with words or symbols. Use at least 4.5:1 contrast for normal text and 3:1 for large text as a conservative reference.
- Keep assumptions and exclusions near affected scope, schedule, or price.

## Template handling

- Preserve supplied DOCX styles, headers, footers, section breaks, and required boilerplate.
- Do not invent a brand when no guide exists; use restrained generic styling.
- Do not overwrite the original template or proposal.
- Render the final DOCX/PDF and inspect every page when layout affects submission.
