# Requirements and evidence

## Source priority

1. RFP, amendments, official Q&A, evaluation sheet, and mandatory template
2. User-approved company facts, policies, contracts, certifications, case studies, and product documents
3. Current authoritative external sources
4. Explicit assumptions awaiting approval

When sources conflict, do not silently choose. Record the conflict and ask for a decision if it affects compliance, price, scope, schedule, or risk.

## Compliance matrix

| ID | Source/location | Requirement | Mandatory | Weight | Response location | Evidence | Owner | State |
|---|---|---|---|---:|---|---|---|---|

- Preserve the requirement's operative words and thresholds.
- Split compound requirements when separate proof or ownership is needed.
- Map source-to-proposal; do not claim compliance because the proposal contains a related topic.
- Record page limits, filenames, signatures, file types, and submission channels as requirements.
- Allocate draft space and review attention according to weight, mandatory status, uncertainty, and delivery risk.

## Claim ledger

| Claim ID | Claim | Source | Version/date | Scope | Proposal locations | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Require evidence for prices, dates, quantities, performance, savings, availability, certifications, compliance, security, lifecycle, support, customer results, competitive comparisons, guarantees, and superlatives.

When evidence is missing, mark `[unverified]`, state an assumption, list an evidence gap, or remove the claim. Never convert uncertainty into confident wording.

## State and approval

- `unanswered`: no response exists
- `drafted`: content exists but has not passed evidence review
- `needs-review`: reviewer action or source confirmation is required
- `approved`: an accountable reviewer accepted content and evidence
- `not-applicable`: a written rationale explains why it does not apply

Finalization requires every mandatory item to be `approved` or justified `not-applicable`, and every sensitive claim to be supported or visibly qualified.
